About Ptitim

Ptitim, also known as Israeli couscous, are tiny toasted pasta pearls invented in the 1950s. They were created as a rice substitute during food shortages in Israel.

Unlike traditional couscous, ptitim are toasted, giving them a unique nutty flavor and chewy texture. They hold their shape well and absorb sauces beautifully, making them versatile in many dishes.

Ptitim can be served hot or cold, as a side dish, in salads, or even as a main course. They are Loved by kids and adults alike, they’re quick to prepare and full of comforting flavor.
